SUN

The Sun is a massive ball of hot gases and the center of our solar system.

Key Facts
  • Age: About 4.6 billion years
  • Temperature: ~5,500°C on the surface
  • Fun Fact: Contains 99.8% of the solar system’s total mass.

EARTH

The only blue planet that supports life.

Key Facts
  • Age: About 4.5 billion years
  • Distance from Sun: ~150 million km
  • Moon: 1 natural satellite
  • Fun Fact: 71% of Earth’s surface is covered by water.

Dwarf Planet

Dwarf planets orbit the Sun but are not dominant in their orbit.

Key Facts
  • Round but not dominant in orbit
  • Located mostly in the Kuiper Belt
  • Fun Fact: Pluto was a planet until 2006.

ASTEROID

Asteroids are rocky objects left from the early formation of our solar system.

Key Facts
  • Main Location: Asteroid Belt
  • Size: Few meters to hundreds of kilometers
  • Largest: Ceres (also a dwarf planet)
  • Fun Fact: Some may contain gold and iron.

KUIPER BELT

A vast ring of icy bodies beyond Neptune.

Key Facts
  • Location: Starts about 30 AU from the Sun
  • Shape: Donut-shaped region
  • Contains: Ice, rock, and frozen gases
  • Fun Fact: Like a “second asteroid belt.”

SPACE PROBES

Robotic spacecraft that explore space beyond Earth’s orbit.

Key Facts
  • Famous: Voyager 1 & 2, New Horizons, Juno
  • No humans onboard — fully robotic
  • Power: Solar or nuclear energy
  • Fun Fact: Voyager 1 is the farthest human-made object in space.

SATELLITE

Objects orbiting planets — natural or man-made for communication and research.

Key Facts
  • Uses: Weather, GPS, communication, imaging
  • Fun Fact: Thousands orbit Earth today.
